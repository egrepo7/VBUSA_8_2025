Volleyball Net Basics

Before diving into product options, it helps to understand the basics:

Standard Size

Most indoor nets have a 2" top and bottom border along with 4" wide side borders. Most outdoor/beach nets feature wider 4" border along all 4 sides which make them easier to see when playing outdoors.

Note: The thicker the border, the heavier the net. For thin poles or lighter carrying weight, we recommend 2" border nets. High-end nets typically have wood dowels sewn into the sides for perfect rectangular hanging.

Net Length & Height

  • Length: Regulation nets are 32 feet long (Most volleyball poles are installed 36 feet apart)
  • Height: High quality nets should be 39 to 41 inches tall
  • Why not 36"? You lose a full 4 inch square of vertical netting, affecting defensive plays

Key Features That Matter

{{!-- Cables & Top/Bottom Lines --}}

1. Cables & Top/Bottom Lines

Galvanized Steel Cable

  • 3,000 lb break point
  • Rust resistant with clear PVC coating
  • Very little stretch when tensioning
  • Theft resistant but harder to roll up
  • Wear safety glasses when handling

Stainless Steel Cable

  • 3,000 lb break point
  • Better for salt water environments
  • Superior appearance for outdoor use
  • Harder to roll up than rope cables
  • Safety glasses recommended

Kevlar Rope Cable

  • 3,000 lb break point
  • Lightweight and easy to handle
  • Used by AVP, colleges, Olympics
  • Easy to roll and tension
  • Gold standard for performance

¼" MFP Rope

  • 1,000 lb break point
  • Braided multi-filament polypropylene
  • Found on budget nets
  • Expected stretching over time
  • Economical option

Why it matters:

Cables keep nets tight and straight — Kevlar is the gold standard for performance, while steel options offer theft resistance and longevity. Custom printing nets can be a good theft deterrent.

Indoor Volleyball Nets

Indoor nets are designed for gyms, schools, and competitive facilities. Standard indoor nets use 2" top/bottom borders for visibility and performance. Many club programs appreciate having their name printed along the top and bottom of the net increasing the border thickness to 4". School programs are only allowed to increase the top tape to 3-½" to allow for printing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between indoor and outdoor nets?

Indoor nets traditionally use a 2" border, while outdoor nets have shifted towards a 4" border for extra player visibility with busy outdoor backgrounds. VolleyballUSA.com produces both indoor and outdoor nets with outdoor rated materials for prolonged usability.

How long will a volleyball net last?

With proper care, indoor nets last several years in gyms. Outdoor nets vary by environment - occasional summer use can last a lifetime, while continuous outdoor exposure typically lasts 3-10 years. High UV areas see faster deterioration. UV protectant sprays from marine supply stores can significantly extend life.

Do I need Kevlar or steel cable?

Kevlar cable nets are much easier to set up than steel cable nets - similar to tying shoe laces. Steel cable nets are sharp, can fray over time, and we recommend wearing safety glasses when setting up. Steel cable nets are harder to cut down if theft is an issue.

Can I replace just the net without buying a full system?

Yes — all nets are sold separately, and many are compatible with existing poles/systems. Check your current pole spacing and mounting hardware to ensure compatibility.

Do you offer custom-printed nets?

Yes! Colleges, cities, homeowners and clubs often use logo-printed nets for branding and sponsorship visibility. Custom printing options are available on many of our professional-grade nets.
